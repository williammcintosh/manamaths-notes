Measurement prefixes

T1_LO9 · lo-yr9-measurement-prefixes

Complete
Key idea:

Metric prefixes scale a unit by powers of 10. \textbf{kilo

means 1000 times bigger, \textbf{centi} means 100 times smaller, and \textbf{milli} means 1000 times smaller.}

Steps

  1. Decide whether you are changing to a bigger or smaller unit.

  1. Use powers of 10: metres $\leftrightarrow$ centimetres $\leftrightarrow$ millimetres.

  1. Multiply when changing to a smaller unit.

  1. Divide when changing to a bigger unit.

Key facts

  • $1 km=1000 m$

  • $1 m=100 cm$

  • $1 cm=10 mm$

  • $1 L=1000 mL$

  • $1 kg=1000 g$

Common mistake

Going the wrong way with place value. Converting metres to centimetres means multiplying by 100, not dividing by 100.

Try these

  1. Convert $3 km$ to metres.

  2. Convert $250 cm$ to metres.

  3. Convert $4 L$ to mL.

Example 1: km to m

Convert $2.5 km$ to metres.$$ 2.5\times1000=2500 m $$

Example 2: cm to m

Convert $450 cm$ to metres.$$ 450\div100=4.5 m $$

Example 3: L to mL

Convert $1.8 L$ to mL.$$ 1.8\times1000=1800 mL $$

Example 4: reasoning

Which is larger: $0.01 km$ or $1000 mm$?$$ 0.01 km=10 m,\quad1000 mm=1 m $$So $0.01 km$ is larger.
